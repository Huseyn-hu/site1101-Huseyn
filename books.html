Books I Have Read

How to Win Friends and Influence People in the Digital Age

Dale Carnegie

Reading

A modern version of Carnegie's classic book that explains how to communicate effectively, build relationships, and create positive influence in online and offline environments. It focuses on empathy, clarity, listening, and ethical influence.

The Opportunity Analysis Canvas

James V. Green

A structured guide used in entrepreneurship courses to understand opportunities, market needs, and personal strengths. It teaches how to analyze ideas, evaluate risks, and develop an entrepreneurial mindset.

The ONE Thing

Gary Keller

A self development book that explains how focusing on the most important task brings clarity, reduces stress, and improves results. It teaches prioritization, productivity, and the value of disciplined focus.

Animal Farm

George Orwell

A political allegory that uses simple storytelling to explore themes of power, control, and leadership. It teaches critical thinking and encourages readers to question systems and authority.

Harry Potter Series

J. K. Rowling

A fantasy series about friendship, courage, personal growth, and the power of choices. It offers imagination and emotional depth and is one of the most influential modern literary works.

The 48 Laws of Power

Robert Greene

A book that analyzes patterns of power, influence, and strategy throughout history. It introduces psychological insights and strategic thinking, often used in leadership and negotiation studies.
